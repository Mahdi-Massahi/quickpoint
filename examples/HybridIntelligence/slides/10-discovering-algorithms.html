While a problem's inherent difficulty is fixed, our classification of it improves as we discover better algorithms.

[[Slide Notes#Discovering more efficient algorithms]]

Let's use Primality testing as an example.

Before 2002, we primarily had probabilistic algorithms (BPP). But with the discovery of the AKS algorithm, we proved it can be solved deterministically in polynomial time (P).

Crucial mindset: These classes are not fixed in our understanding. Progress in Computer Science often means finding a "shortcut" that moves a problem from a hard class to an easier one.
